The Sight-Size Method & Classical Tradition

At the Ottawa Creative Academy, we preserve the rigorous drawing and painting traditions of the 19th-century European ateliers. Our foundational instruction is rooted in the **Sight-Size method**—a drafting technique where the subject and the drawing are viewed from a set distance at a 1:1 scale comparison. This allows the artist to measure proportions with absolute accuracy and train the eye to perceive subtle value gradients.
By working sequentially from plaster casts to figure studies and eventually oil painting, students master comparative measurement, value grouping, and edge control. We believe that creative expression is unlocked only when the artist gains complete command over their drawing and painting mediums.
Instructor Spotlight

Anselme Massenat

Founder & Principal Instructor

Anselme Massenat founded the Ottawa Creative Academy with the goal of providing structured, academic drawing and painting instruction in Canada's capital. Having trained in traditional European ateliers, Anselme specializes in classical portraiture, figure drawing, and cast study methodologies.
His teaching style combines rigorous, step-by-step demonstrations with highly detailed, individual critiques. "Our objective is not to teach a single style," says Anselme, "but to provide students with the optical tools and technical foundations they need to accurately record any subject."
